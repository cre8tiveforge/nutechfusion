*New Hope Methodist Church is a small but active community church known for its welcoming atmosphere and outreach. It serves families and individuals of all ages, offering a place to worship, learn, and connect with others in faith.*

## **History & Mission**

**History:** New Hope Methodist Church was founded in the mid-1980s by a handful of local families seeking a Methodist congregation in their neighborhood. The church began in a rented chapel and moved to its current Oakwood Drive location in 1990\. Over the years, it has grown into a close-knit community hub, **playing an active role in local outreach and charity**. Many long-time Houston residents recall New Hope’s involvement in food drives, disaster relief efforts, and neighborhood events. Despite its growth, the church has maintained a “small church family” feel where everyone knows each other by name.

**Mission & Values:** The core mission of New Hope Methodist Church is *“to be a beacon of hope by sharing the love of Christ through worship, service, and community.”* This means the church focuses on heartfelt worship, compassionate service to others, and building loving community relationships. Its core values include: faith in Jesus Christ, inclusive hospitality (welcoming all with open hearts), outreach and service to those in need, spiritual growth through discipleship, and nurturing families and youth. The congregation lives by the United Methodist ethos of *“open hearts, open minds, open doors,”* striving to **welcome everyone** and guide each person to grow in faith. New Hope regularly partners with community organizations to put these values into action, reflecting its commitment to both *loving God* and *loving neighbor* in tangible ways.

**Leadership Structure:** New Hope Methodist Church operates under a church council model common to Methodist congregations. The **Church Council** (elected from members) meets regularly to make decisions on church matters, set vision, and oversee committees. Key committees include:

* **Trustees Committee:** Responsible for church property and maintenance of the facilities.  
* **Finance Committee:** Oversees budgeting, finances, and stewardship campaigns.  
* **Staff-Parish Relations Committee (SPRC):** Acts as a human resources committee, supporting the staff and pastors and liaising with the regional Methodist conference.  
* **Missions/Outreach Committee:** Plans community service projects, charity drives, and mission donations.  
* **Worship Committee:** Assists with planning worship services, special services, and evaluating the worship needs of the congregation.

There is also a designated **Lay Leader**, a church member who works alongside the pastors to represent the congregation and encourage lay (non-clergy) involvement. New Hope does not have formal elders or deacons like some denominations; instead, dedicated members and volunteers serve in leadership roles through these committees and ministry teams. **Decision-making is collaborative** – the pastor(s) and lay leadership prayerfully work together to guide the church’s ministries.

## **Worship Services & Schedule**

* **Sunday Worship Service:** *Sundays at 10:30 AM.* This is the main weekly worship gathering for the whole church. The service is a **blended worship style** – you can expect a mix of traditional hymns (often led by the choir or piano) and contemporary praise songs (led by the guitar/keyboard-based worship band). Worship usually begins with uplifting music and prayer, followed by scripture readings and a sermon. Pastor John’s preaching is Bible-based, conversational, and relevant to everyday life, typically lasting about 20-25 minutes. The atmosphere is reverent but relaxed; laughter, “Amen” responses, and personal stories often find their way into the service, making it engaging for all ages.

* **Sunday Morning Schedule:** *Sundays before worship.* In addition to the 10:30 worship service, the church offers **Sunday School classes at 9:15 AM** for all ages (see the “Classes & Groups” section below for details). Many members arrive early for these classes and then gather for a brief fellowship/coffee time around 10:15 AM before heading into the sanctuary. **Nursery care** for infants and toddlers is available starting at 9:15 and continues through the worship hour so parents can attend classes and service (more on childcare in the FAQs section).

* **Midweek Prayer & Bible Study:** *Wednesdays at 7:00 PM.* Every Wednesday evening, members gather in the Fellowship Hall (or a small chapel room) for a midweek **Prayer Service and Bible Study**. This is an informal hour-long gathering including group prayer (for personal needs, the community, and global concerns) and an interactive Bible study discussion led by Pastor John or a rotating lay teacher. It’s a time for spiritual refreshment in the middle of the week. On the first Wednesday of each month, this midweek time is dedicated to a **church-wide prayer meeting**, focusing on specific prayer themes or needs in the community.

* **Online Services:** New Hope makes its worship accessible online for those who cannot attend in person. The **10:30 AM Sunday service is live-streamed** on the church’s Facebook page and on the church website. Sermon videos (and audio recordings) are archived on the website so people can watch or listen later. In addition, during special circumstances (like severe weather or public health concerns), the church has the capability to hold services and Bible studies via Zoom or Facebook Live. The aim is to keep everyone connected spiritually even when they’re physically apart. Virtual attendees are encouraged to check in online, and there’s a live-chat host on Facebook during the service to greet and interact with those watching from home.

* **Special Worship Services:** Throughout the year, New Hope holds special services for important days in the Christian calendar. For example, a **Christmas Eve Candlelight Service** is held on December 24 at 7:00 PM, featuring carols, the Christmas story, and the beautiful tradition of singing “Silent Night” by candlelight. During Holy Week before Easter, the church typically offers a **Good Friday evening service** and sometimes a casual **Sunrise Service on Easter Sunday** (often outdoors, weather permitting, around 6:30 AM) in addition to the regular worship time. These special services are announced in advance and are open to all, including visitors.

*(Worship at New Hope is family-friendly and welcoming. Children and youth participate in the first part of worship and then may go to age-specific lessons, or they can stay with their families—whichever parents prefer. Services usually last about 1 hour. There is fellowship time with coffee and snacks after service, giving people a chance to chat and connect.)*

## **Facility Layout & Key Areas**

New Hope Methodist Church’s building is modest in size but well-utilized. Here’s an overview of the main areas of the facility and what you’ll find in each:

* **Sanctuary:** The Sanctuary is the main worship space where Sunday services are held. It can seat approximately 150 people in wooden pews arranged in a semi-circle. The décor is simple and inviting: a cross and altar table at the front, stained-glass windows along the sides (depicting scenes like the Good Shepherd and the empty tomb), and a small choir loft to the right of the platform. The chancel (stage area) has a pulpit, lectern, and space for musical instruments (there’s a piano and an electronic keyboard, plus guitar stands and drums for the worship band). The sanctuary is equipped with a sound system and projection screen for song lyrics and sermon notes. **Accessibility:** There are wide side aisles for wheelchair access and reserved seating at the back for those with mobility aids if needed.

* **Fellowship Hall:** Adjacent to the sanctuary (just through a set of double doors) is the Fellowship Hall. This multi-purpose hall is basically the church’s **gathering and event space**. It has about a dozen round tables and chairs that can be arranged for different functions – from after-service coffee hour to potluck meals, meetings, and seasonal events. A small stage on one end of the hall is used for presentations or performances (the children’s Christmas pageant is held here, for example). The Fellowship Hall is connected to a **kitchen**, fully stocked with appliances, dishes, and a serving counter, which makes it easy to host dinners and community meals. The hall can accommodate 100+ people for dining or auditorium-style seating. It’s a bustling place after church on Sundays, as many stay for refreshments, and it’s also home to midweek dinners, youth game nights, and the annual fall festival.

* **Classrooms:** Down the hallway from the Fellowship Hall, you’ll find several classrooms and meeting rooms. **Sunday School classes** and small group Bible studies meet in these rooms. For instance, one classroom is designated for children’s Sunday School (decorated with colorful Bible posters and stocked with craft supplies), another room serves as the **Youth Room** (with couches, a ping-pong table, and a whiteboard – a casual space for teens to hang out and have lessons), and one or two rooms are set up for adult classes or meetings (with tables, chairs, and a TV or projector for video studies). During the week, these classrooms also double as meeting spaces for committees, Bible study groups, or community groups that use the church. Each room has signage by the door, and a central hallway bulletin board displays a map and schedule of what happens in each room on Sundays.

* **Offices:** The church office suite is located near the main entrance. It includes the **Pastor’s Study** (Pastor John’s office), an **Assistant Pastor/Youth Pastor Office** (shared by Rev. Lisa and some volunteers), and a small **administrative office** where Michael (the administrator) works. The Pastor’s Study is a cozy room with bookshelves full of Bibles, commentaries, and community photos; it also has a small meeting table for counseling sessions or meetings with families. The admin office is essentially the “front desk” of the church—if you visit on a weekday, that’s where you’ll be greeted. Office hours are typically Monday–Thursday, 9:00 AM to 3:00 PM. There’s also a workroom with a copier, storage for supplies, and a mail area for church leaders. The offices serve as the coordination center for all church operations and are clearly marked so visitors can find help or information easily.

* **Nursery & Children’s Area:** For families with young kids, New Hope provides a safe and fun children’s area. The **Nursery** is a brightly painted room near the sanctuary entrance, staffed on Sunday mornings. It has cribs, rocking chairs, plenty of toys, and a secure check-in/check-out system for parents dropping off infants (0–2 years) and toddlers. Adjacent to the nursery is a **Children’s Church room** (or “Kids’ Corner”) used during part of the worship service for ages 3-7. This room has child-sized tables and chairs, story time rugs, and a puppet theater stage – it’s where volunteers lead Bible stories, songs, and crafts for young children after the children’s message in the main service. Both the nursery and children’s rooms are cleaned regularly and adhere to the church’s child safety policies. Parents can be confident that their little ones are well cared for while they attend worship.

* **Parking:** New Hope has its own **parking lot** surrounding the church building, with approximately 60 parking spaces. There are clearly marked **visitor parking** spots near the front entrance and several **accessible parking** spots with ramp access to the main doors. On a typical Sunday, the lot has ample space for attendees, but during big events (like Easter or Christmas services), overflow parking is available along the residential street or at a neighboring school parking lot with a short walk. The church provides signage and sometimes volunteer parking attendants during major events to help direct traffic. The lot is well-lit for evening activities, and there are bike racks near the side entrance for those who bike to church. Overall, accessing the church is convenient whether you drive or walk from the surrounding neighborhood.

*(In summary, the facility is easy to navigate, with all main areas on one level. Restrooms are located in the main hallway (including a wheelchair-accessible/family restroom). Signs throughout the building help newcomers find their way, and any usher or member would be happy to point you in the right direction.)*

## **Regularly Scheduled Classes & Groups**

New Hope Methodist Church encourages everyone to grow in their faith and build relationships through various classes and small groups. Here are the **regular classes and fellowship groups** and when they typically meet:

* **Sunday School (All Ages):** Every Sunday at 9:15 AM (before the worship service), the church offers Sunday School classes:

  * *Children’s Sunday School:* Kids ages 4–11 meet in the Children’s Classroom for Bible stories, songs, and crafts. Lessons follow a kid-friendly curriculum that introduces Bible characters and themes in fun ways. (Location: Children’s classroom, next to the nursery.)  
  * *Youth Sunday School:* Middle and high school students (ages 12–18) gather in the Youth Room. These sessions are interactive and often discussion-based, tackling how biblical lessons apply to school and teen life. The youth enjoy donuts or snacks as they chat about faith with Youth Director Rev. Lisa or another youth leader.  
  * *Adult Sunday School:* Adults have their own class (or sometimes two classes) that meet in a classroom or the Fellowship Hall. One popular class is a Bible study that works through a book of the Bible or a topical study (e.g., Christian living, prayer, etc.), led by a knowledgeable church member or the pastor. Another class, when offered, might be a *Newcomers/Explorers Class* that covers Methodist basics and is great for visitors or new members. Adults of all ages are welcome, and it’s a casual discussion format—coffee is usually provided\!  
* **Men’s Bible Study:** A group of men from the church meets weekly for study, fellowship, and support. **When:** Wednesday mornings at 6:30 AM at a local diner *or* Saturday mornings at 8:00 AM at the church (the schedule can vary by season, but currently it’s Saturdays). The men’s group reads through a book of the Bible or a Christian book and discusses how it applies to their roles as husbands, fathers, employees, and friends. They also pray for one another. It’s an informal, come-as-you-are group; even if you’re not a morning person, the coffee and camaraderie help wake you up\! Periodically, the Men’s Ministry also hosts a Saturday **men’s breakfast** in the Fellowship Hall with a guest speaker.

* **Women’s Bible Study:** The women of New Hope have a dedicated Bible study group as well. **When:** Thursday evenings at 7:00 PM in one of the church classrooms. This group provides a chance for women to connect, share, and grow in faith together. They often use a structured study guide or video series (for example, a Beth Moore or Priscilla Shirer study) focusing on topics like prayer, finding peace, or women of the Bible. All ages are welcome, from young adult women to seniors, bringing a lovely mix of perspectives. Childcare can be arranged if needed for those attending. The Women’s Ministry also plans occasional **Ladies’ Night Out** fellowship events and an annual **women’s retreat** (a one-day getaway for worship and teaching).

* **Youth Group:** Beyond Sunday School, the church’s teens get together for Youth Group. **When:** Wednesday nights at 7:00 PM (concurrent with the adult prayer meeting, but the youth have their own program). Under Rev. Lisa’s leadership, the youth group meeting includes games or icebreakers, a relevant devotional or Bible lesson, and group discussion on real-life issues (school, friendships, faith challenges). They also plan fun outings like bowling, laser tag, or volunteer projects such as helping at the food pantry. The youth often participate in larger events like summer church camp, youth rallies, and a yearly mission trip or service week. New Hope’s youth group is a welcoming place for any teen looking for friendship and positive support.

* **Children’s Ministry Programs:** In addition to Sunday School, children have other opportunities:

  * **Children’s Church:** During the Sunday sermon time, kids ages 3–7 can attend Children’s Church (in the children’s area) for a kid-tailored Bible lesson and activity. (This is optional; children are also welcome to stay with family in worship.)  
  * **Midweek Kids’ Club:** On the second Wednesday of each month at 7:00 PM, while adults have Bible study, children (up to 5th grade) can join a Kids’ Club in the fellowship hall or playground. This club night features a short Bible story, then games, crafts, or music practice (for example, preparing a song to sing in an upcoming service). It’s supervised by children’s ministry volunteers and gives kids a fun midweek faith boost.  
  * **Nursery Playgroup:** For parents with infants/toddlers, the church hosts a casual playgroup on Friday mornings at 10:00 AM (frequency: bi-weekly). Parents or caregivers chat and support each other while little ones play in the nursery or on the church playground.  
* **Small Groups (Home Groups):** New Hope supports a few small groups that meet in homes for those who want a more intimate fellowship setting. These are often called **Life Groups** or **Home Bible Studies**. For example, one group meets Sunday evenings at 6:00 PM at a member’s home in Pearland (a suburb) for potluck dinner and a study on Christian parenting. Another group meets Monday nights at 7:00 PM on Zoom (which started during COVID and continues for convenience) for topical studies and prayer. These groups typically consist of 6–12 people and are a great way to form close friendships. New Hope encourages members to join a small group where they can “do life together,” praying and caring for one another beyond Sunday.

* **Discipleship & Confirmation Classes:** Periodically, the church offers short-term classes for deeper discipleship:

  * A **New Members Class** (also known as **Methodist 101**) is offered twice a year (for example, a 4-week class on Sunday afternoons) to help new attendees understand the church’s beliefs, Methodism, and ways to get involved (details also in the Membership section).  
  * **Confirmation Class:** For youth (usually around 6th-8th grade) interested in becoming full church members, the pastor leads a 8-10 week confirmation course every spring. This class teaches the basics of Christian faith and Methodist tradition, culminating in a special confirmation Sunday where the youth are received as members.  
  * **Topical Classes:** Occasionally, there are classes like a Financial Peace University course (teaching biblical financial principles) or a Parenting Workshop series on Wednesday nights for a set duration. These are announced in the church bulletin and open to members and the community.

*(This variety of classes and groups means there’s something for everyone—children, youth, men, women, and mixed groups—throughout the week. Schedules are posted on the church website calendar and in the Sunday bulletin, and new participants are always welcome to drop in.)*

## **Community Outreach & Events**

Serving the community is at the heart of New Hope’s identity. The church organizes and participates in numerous outreach programs and special events to bless others and build relationships. Here are some key community-focused ministries and annual events:

* **Food Pantry Ministry:** New Hope Methodist operates a small **Food Pantry** that provides free groceries and essentials to families in need. The pantry is open **every Saturday from 9:00 AM to 11:00 AM** in the Fellowship Hall. Church volunteers stock the pantry with canned goods, fresh produce, and toiletries donated by members and partner organizations. Those in the community facing hardship can come by (no appointment needed) and receive a bag or two of groceries, along with a warm cup of coffee and a friendly conversation. This ministry has been running for over a decade and serves dozens of households each month. Volunteers are always welcome – tasks include sorting food, greeting guests, or delivering items to homebound neighbors. Especially around the holidays, the food pantry expands to include turkey or ham giveaways at Thanksgiving and Christmas food baskets.

* **Community Meal Program:** Once a month, New Hope hosts a **free Community Dinner** (often on the last Friday of the month at 6:30 PM). This event is open to anyone – church members, neighbors, and those who might appreciate a hot meal and company. Members of the congregation take turns cooking a large meal (like spaghetti, chili, or barbecue), and everyone shares the meal in the Fellowship Hall. The goal is to build relationships and ensure no one in the community eats alone if they don’t want to. It’s common to see a diverse group of people dining together, from elderly locals to families with kids. Sometimes a short devotional or live music is offered during the dinner, but mostly it’s about fellowship. The community dinner program has been a great success in breaking down barriers and showing hospitality.

* **Clothing Drives and Thrift Outreach:** Twice a year (Spring and Fall), the church holds a **Clothing Drive** where members donate gently used clothes, shoes, and coats. These items are then made available to those in need during a Saturday “free shop” event. In addition, New Hope partners with a local non-profit thrift store – members volunteer there monthly to help sort donations, and in return the thrift store gives vouchers to distribute to low-income individuals from the church’s outreach programs.

* **Holiday Outreach Events:** During major holidays, New Hope plans special outreach initiatives:

  * **Easter Outreach:** Each year before Easter, the church organizes a **Community Easter Egg Hunt** at a nearby park or on the church lawn. Children from the church and neighborhood hunt for eggs, enjoy face-painting and games, and hear a short, kid-friendly Easter message. It’s a fun, free event that often draws many young families who might not otherwise attend church. Additionally, the church delivers Easter baskets to a nearby children’s shelter as part of its outreach.  
  * **Thanksgiving Outreach:** In November, church volunteers put together **Thanksgiving meal baskets** (with turkey, stuffing, and sides) for families who might struggle to afford a big holiday meal. Names are gathered through the food pantry and local school counselors, and baskets are delivered the week of Thanksgiving. On Thanksgiving Day, some members also join with other local churches to serve dinner at a downtown homeless mission.  
  * **Christmas Outreach:** New Hope participates in **Operation Christmas Child** (filling shoeboxes with gifts for children overseas) and hosts an **“Angel Tree” gift program**, where members buy Christmas gifts for children in need or local foster children. The church also visits a local nursing home in December – the choir and youth will sing carols and hand out small gifts to the residents, spreading Christmas cheer.  
* **Vacation Bible School (VBS):** Each summer, typically in June or July, New Hope runs a **Vacation Bible School** program for one week in the evenings. VBS is a free week-long camp for kids (Pre-K through 5th grade) filled with **Bible stories, music, games, and crafts** around a fun theme (like “Adventure Island” or “Space Quest for Jesus”). Many children from the community attend even if they aren’t regular churchgoers. Teenagers and adults from the church volunteer as group leaders and helpers. VBS week is one of the biggest outreach events of the year, often drawing 50-100 children. It ends with a special Sunday service where the kids share songs they learned and display their craft projects, and parents are invited to experience the welcoming environment at New Hope.

* **Back-to-School Fair:** In August, right before school starts, New Hope partners with other area churches to host a **Backpack & School Supplies Giveaway**. The event at New Hope’s Fellowship Hall provides free backpacks filled with pencils, notebooks, and other supplies to students from low-income families. There are also stations for free haircuts, vision screenings, and a prayer table where families can receive encouragement for the new school year. This event is another way New Hope supports local children and builds goodwill in the community.

* **Volunteer Opportunities:** The church offers many **hands-on volunteer opportunities** for members who want to serve. Some examples include: preparing or serving food at the community dinners or pantry, tutoring or reading to children during an after-school program that New Hope assists at a nearby elementary school, participating in neighborhood clean-up days, or joining the **Habitat for Humanity** build team (New Hope sends volunteers to Habitat builds a few Saturdays a year). Whether someone has a lot of time or just an occasional free weekend, there are ways to get involved in serving others. The Outreach Committee keeps a calendar of volunteer events and regularly announces needs in the bulletin and on social media.

* **Partnerships with Local Charities:** New Hope believes in collaboration, so it partners with several local organizations:

  * It has a close relationship with the **Houston Food Bank** (supplying bulk food for the pantry).  
  * Volunteers from New Hope regularly help at the **Star of Hope Mission** (a local homeless shelter) by serving meals or mentoring residents.  
  * The church supports a missionary couple working with orphanages in Central America and has a **“Sister Church” partnership** with a small Methodist congregation in Honduras, exchanging letters and occasional visit trips.  
  * New Hope is also an active member of the **Houston Area Methodist Ministries**, joining city-wide projects like flood relief and inter-church worship events.

*(All these outreach activities and events are publicized through the church’s channels, and everyone is encouraged to take part. They reflect New Hope’s heart for being not just a church **in** the community, but a church **for** the community.)*

## **Special Programs & Ministries**

In addition to regular services and outreach, New Hope Methodist Church offers a variety of special ministries that cater to specific interests and needs:

* **Music Ministry:** Music is an integral part of New Hope’s worship and community life. The **Adult Choir** meets for rehearsal every Wednesday at 6:00 PM (before the prayer meeting) to practice hymns and special anthems. This choir sings most Sundays at the 10:30 service (especially in the traditional segments) and performs cantatas for Christmas and Easter. There’s also a **Praise Band** comprised of musicians (guitarists, a keyboardist, a drummer, and vocalists) who lead contemporary worship songs; they practice on Tuesday nights at 7:00 PM. New Hope encourages those with musical gifts to join – whether it’s singing, playing an instrument, or even helping with sound equipment. For children, the church occasionally runs a **Children’s Choir** or kids’ music time during the Sunday School hour, so they can sing a song in a service or at the Christmas pageant. Additionally, the **Handbell Choir** is a seasonal group (formed around Christmas) that practices handbell ringing and adds a beautiful sound to holiday services. The music ministry isn’t just about performances – members see it as a way to **worship God and inspire the congregation** through song.

* **Missions and Mission Trips:** New Hope has a strong mission focus both locally and abroad. Locally, as noted, they support shelters and food programs. On a broader scale, the church organizes **short-term mission trips** or service trips about once a year. For example, a team from New Hope might spend a week in the summer volunteering in a Gulf Coast town rebuilding homes after a hurricane, or travel to Mexico or Central America on a mission trip to assist a church or orphanage (sometimes done in partnership with other Methodist churches). Youth often participate in these trips as part of their summer program – one year, the high school youth went to Oklahoma to help repair homes on a Native American reservation. The Missions Committee helps select and plan these trips. **Fundraising events** like bake sales or car washes are held to support travel expenses, and the whole congregation prays for and follows the work of the mission teams. These experiences often ignite a deeper passion for service in those who go, and they come back sharing testimonies that inspire the church.

* **Counseling Services:** While New Hope does not have a licensed counselor on staff, it does offer **pastoral counseling** and referrals. Pastor John is available by appointment to counsel individuals, couples, or families dealing with personal issues, marriage challenges, grief, or spiritual concerns. His approach is supportive and faith-based; he will listen, pray, and offer guidance from a Christian perspective. For more specialized or long-term needs, the church keeps a referral list of trusted Christian counselors and therapists in the Houston area and can help connect members to professional counseling (for example, therapists who handle mental health issues, addiction recovery, or family therapy). The church also periodically hosts seminars or workshops on topics like **marriage enrichment**, parenting, or financial management, which have a coaching/counseling aspect. These might be one-day events or short series led by guest speakers. All counseling-related conversations at New Hope are kept confidential, and the church strives to provide a safe, non-judgmental environment for people seeking help.

* **Support Groups:** New Hope provides and supports a variety of support groups to care for the emotional and spiritual needs of its congregation and community:

  * **Grief Recovery Group:** Several times a year (often in the spring and fall), the church offers a grief support class, such as the well-known **GriefShare** program. This is a 8-10 week small-group series for anyone who has lost a loved one and is navigating the grieving process. Participants watch a video, then discuss and share with others who understand, finding comfort and hope together. These sessions are typically led by a trained facilitator from the church who has experienced loss as well.  
  * **Addiction Support:** New Hope hosts a weekly **Alcoholics Anonymous (AA)** meeting on Monday nights at 7:30 PM in one of the classrooms. This open meeting welcomes both church members and community folks dealing with alcoholism to find support in sobriety. While AA is not run by the church per se, providing the space is one way New Hope supports addiction recovery. Additionally, the church is open to those seeking spiritual help in overcoming addictions – the pastor and lay leaders will pray with individuals and connect them to rehab or counseling resources. There has been interest in starting a **Celebrate Recovery** ministry (a Christian 12-step program addressing various hurts, habits, and hang-ups) and the leadership is exploring training for that in the future.  
  * **Marriage Enrichment:** For couples looking to strengthen their marriage, New Hope occasionally runs a **marriage enrichment small group** or retreat. For example, a 6-week “Love and Respect” video-based class was held last year on Sunday evenings, which many couples found helpful. Also, every February around Valentine’s Day, the church hosts a **“Couples Date Night”** event – an evening with dinner, child care provided, and a short encouraging message or testimony on marriage. It’s both fun and uplifting, aimed at helping couples invest in their relationship with support from the church community.  
  * **Other Groups:** If enough interest arises, the church is willing to facilitate other support or interest-based groups. There has been a **Moms’ Group** (for young mothers) that meets informally for playdates and prayer, and a few members have started a **Walking Club** that meets Saturday mornings at a local park for exercise and fellowship. The motto is: if you have a need or passion, New Hope wants to help you find others to share it with\!  
* **Special Interest Ministries:**

  * **Seasonal Programs:** The church frequently organizes seasonal programs such as a **Christmas Pageant** (children and youth put on a Nativity play or Christmas musical each December) and **Easter Sunrise Service** (as mentioned, often followed by a pancake breakfast cooked by the men’s group). These events involve a lot of volunteers – from set designers to cooks – creating additional ways to serve.  
  * **Prayer Chain & Prayer Team:** Behind the scenes, New Hope has a dedicated **Prayer Ministry**. A group of prayer warriors maintains a “prayer chain” (via email and phone) to lift up urgent prayer requests as they come in. Also, a small team meets Sunday mornings at 8:45 AM specifically to pray over the day’s services, the pastors, and any known needs. Members can submit prayer requests through a card on Sunday or online, and this team confidentially prays for each request.  
  * **Technology & Media Ministry:** A few tech-savvy members manage the **audio-visual system** during services (sound board, slide projection) and run the **live-stream**. They also maintain the church website and social media updates with sermon podcasts, event photos, and announcements. This behind-the-scenes ministry has become increasingly important for reaching people online.

*(New Hope’s special programs are driven by the interests and needs of its members. The church leadership is open to new ministries; for instance, if someone has a heart for prison ministry or a talent like teaching art, they are encouraged to form a group or outreach. This flexibility means the list of ministries can grow and change as God leads the community.)*

## **Membership & How to Get Involved**

Whether you are a long-time attendee or brand new, New Hope Methodist Church welcomes you to take the next steps in deeper involvement. Here’s how you can become a member and get plugged into serving and fellowship:

* **Becoming a Member:** Official membership at New Hope (and in the United Methodist Church) is open to anyone who professes faith in Jesus Christ. The process is straightforward and friendly. If you’re interested in joining, you can **talk with Pastor John** or **attend a New Members Class**. The New Members Class is a short orientation (usually a one-day seminar or two Sunday afternoons) where you’ll learn about the church’s beliefs, the Methodist heritage, and the expectations of membership (such as supporting the church through your prayers, presence, gifts, service, and witness). After that, you can choose to take membership vows during a Sunday service – this is a short ceremony where new members are introduced to the congregation, answer a few affirmations of faith, and are warmly welcomed (often with handshakes or applause, and a welcome luncheon after). If you haven’t been baptized, baptism can be arranged as part of joining (Methodists accept infant baptism or adult baptism from any Christian denomination, so if you were baptized previously, that counts). Membership is basically a commitment to make New Hope your church home, but **everyone is welcome** at worship and events even if they are not official members yet.

* **Joining Ministries & Volunteer Teams:** One of the best ways to feel a part of the church family is to volunteer or join a ministry team. New Hope has many opportunities to serve, and they encourage members to use their gifts and passions. Here are some ways to get involved:

  * *Worship & Music:* Join the choir or praise team if you sing or play an instrument, volunteer as an usher or greeter on Sundays (welcoming people, handing out bulletins, helping with offering collection), or assist on the tech team if you have AV skills.  
  * *Children & Youth:* Help in the nursery or teach a children’s Sunday School class (background check required and training provided), volunteer with the youth group (as a chaperone for events or a mentor), or assist with Vacation Bible School in the summer.  
  * *Hospitality & Fellowship:* Sign up to bring snacks for coffee hour, help organize potlucks and dinners, or join the care team that delivers meals to members recovering from illness/surgery.  
  * *Outreach & Missions:* Lend a hand at the Saturday food pantry, serve on the Outreach/Missions Committee to plan community service projects, participate in mission trips or local volunteer days, or be a liaison with one of our charity partners.  
  * *Prayer & Care:* Join the prayer chain or volunteer to visit homebound members and those in the hospital (Pastor John often brings a lay member along for visitations).  
  * *Property & Maintenance:* If you’re handy, the Trustees welcome help on church workdays (fixing, cleaning, gardening on the grounds) or specific projects like setting up decorations for holidays.  
* New Hope often announces specific volunteer needs in the bulletin and monthly newsletter. To get involved, you can fill out a **“I Want to Serve” card** available in the lobby or on the website, indicating your areas of interest. Ministry leaders will reach out and get you plugged in. Serving not only helps others, but it’s also a great way to build friendships and grow in faith as you work alongside fellow church members.

* **Giving & Donations:** New Hope Methodist Church is supported by the generous giving of its members and attendees. There are several ways to give:

  * *In Person:* During each Sunday service, an offering is collected. People can give via cash or check in the offering plate. (Envelopes are available for confidentiality, and receipts for tax-deductible giving are provided annually.)  
  * *Online:* The church website has a secure **online giving portal**, allowing one-time or recurring donations using a credit/debit card or bank transfer. Many members appreciate the convenience of setting up monthly giving online.  
  * *By Mail:* Donations can be mailed to the church office (attention: Treasurer) if you prefer.  
  * *Special Offerings:* A few times a year, the church participates in special offerings for the denomination (such as UMC’s World Communion Sunday offering, or disaster relief funds when crises happen). These are announced in advance and are always optional.  
* Every contribution, large or small, goes toward supporting the church’s ministries, maintaining the facilities, paying staff, and funding outreach efforts. Transparency is valued – the Finance Committee provides financial updates, and an annual budget is approved by the Church Council. Members are encouraged to practice stewardship as part of their discipleship, but **there is no obligation or pressure to give** for visitors. New Hope teaches that giving should be done cheerfully and as one is able (2 Corinthians 9:7). Even non-monetary contributions (like donating supplies or volunteering time) are considered valuable ways to give.

* **Fellowship and Social Activities:** Being involved isn’t just about “work” — it’s also about forming community. New Hope has various fellowship activities like **family game nights**, holiday parties (e.g., a Christmas decorating party where everyone trims the church tree together), and after-church lunches at local restaurants. Newcomers are always invited and welcomed to these gatherings. By showing up and participating, you quickly become part of the New Hope family. Don’t hesitate to introduce yourself to the pastors or ministry leaders — they love getting to know new folks and will help you find your place in the church community.

*(In summary, getting involved at New Hope can be as simple as showing up, raising your hand to help, or taking that step of joining officially. The church’s motto for involvement could be, “You belong here, and we have a place for you to serve and grow\!”)*

## **FAQs (Frequently Asked Questions)**

**Q: What is the dress code?**  
 **A:** *There is no strict dress code at New Hope.* You’ll find people wearing anything from casual attire to their “Sunday best.” On a typical Sunday, most attendees dress in a comfortable, casual or business-casual style (for example, jeans or khakis with a polo or blouse, or a simple dress). A few might wear a suit or tie, and others might wear t-shirts – it’s truly “come as you are.” The church’s priority is that you feel welcome and not worried about your outfit. So whether you prefer to dress up or keep it casual, you will fit in just fine.

**Q: Is the church accessible for people with disabilities?**  
 **A:** Yes. New Hope Methodist Church is **wheelchair accessible** and strives to accommodate everyone’s needs. The main entrance has a ramp, and all facilities (sanctuary, fellowship hall, restrooms, etc.) are on one ground level with no stairs. There are a few reserved parking spots near the entrance for those with mobility challenges. Inside the sanctuary, there is space to accommodate wheelchairs or walkers at the end of pew rows, and ushers are happy to assist with any special seating needs. The church also offers **hearing assistance devices** during worship for those who are hard of hearing – just ask an usher for a headset. Additionally, the sound system includes a hearing loop compatible with many hearing aids. For visually impaired individuals, large-print bulletins or hymn lyrics can be provided if requested. The church leadership is open to making further accommodations as needed (such as arranging ASL interpretation for the deaf if a need arises). In summary, New Hope aims to be welcoming and accessible to all, regardless of physical ability.

**Q: Are there childcare options during services?**  
 **A:** Yes, childcare is provided to ensure families can worship comfortably. **During the 10:30 AM Sunday service,** a staffed nursery is available for infants and toddlers (up to about 3 years old). Parents can sign their little ones into the nursery before service; the nursery is supervised by trained, background-checked volunteers who will rock, play with, or soothe your child as needed. For preschoolers and early elementary kids (approximately ages 3–7), there is an option partway through the service to go to **Children’s Church** – a special time where kids have a Bible story and activity tailored to their age. The children start in the sanctuary with their families and usually depart for Children’s Church after the opening songs and a short kids’ message/blessing from the pastor. Older children and teenagers generally stay in the service (and we love having them participate), but there are **activity packets** with coloring sheets and crayons available at the welcome table for any kids sitting through the sermon. Additionally, Sunday School classes for all children and youth are offered at 9:15 AM before worship, as described earlier. For parents who choose to keep their children with them during worship, that is completely okay too – the sounds of children are considered a joy, not a distraction. The church is accustomed to kids and understands that little ones might wiggle or make noise. There’s also a **cry room/quiet room** at the back of the sanctuary with a window and speaker, in case a parent needs to step out with a fussy baby while still listening to the service. In short, New Hope is very family-friendly and provides flexible options so parents can engage in worship knowing their children are well cared for.

**Q: I’m new. What should I expect when I visit for the first time?**  
 **A:** First of all, *welcome\!* Visiting New Hope, you can expect a warm, friendly experience. Here’s a quick rundown:

* **Arrival:** When you arrive, greeters at the door will say hello and hand you a bulletin (which outlines the service and upcoming events). They’ll happily answer any immediate questions (like where the restrooms are or where to take your children). If you have kids, the greeter can direct you to the nursery or a Sunday School class if you’re there early.  
* **Seating:** Feel free to sit anywhere you like in the sanctuary. Members do not have “assigned seats,” and newcomers are encouraged to make themselves comfortable. Ushers are on hand if you need help finding a seat or if you have special needs.  
* **During Service:** The service itself will include some singing (lyrics are projected on a screen and also often in the bulletin), prayers, scripture readings, and a sermon. If you’re unfamiliar with the order of worship, don’t worry — the pastor or worship leader usually gives cues, and you can follow along in the bulletin. There’s usually a time where attendees briefly greet one another (“passing the peace” or a simple hello handshake). As a visitor, you can participate as much or as little as you feel comfortable. You will not be singled out or asked to introduce yourself in the service, so you can relax and just experience it.  
* **Offering:** An offering plate is passed during the service for regular attenders to support the church. As a visitor, you’re not expected to give anything, so feel free to just pass the plate along. There is also a **Connection Card** in the pews or given with your bulletin – if you’re willing, you can fill it out with your name and contact info. This lets the church know you visited and if you want more information. You can drop the card in the offering plate or hand it to a greeter after service. (This is totally optional but helps the church follow up with a thank-you note or more info.)  
* **After Service:** After the worship concludes (around 11:30 AM), many people stick around for a few minutes to chat. There is usually **coffee and refreshments** in the Fellowship Hall right after service – you’re invited to grab a cup and a cookie and meet a few folks. Don’t hesitate to introduce yourself to Pastor John or Pastor Lisa; they would love to meet you and answer any questions you have. They usually stand by the main exit to greet everyone as they leave.  
* **Atmosphere:** Overall, expect a casual, *come-as-you-are* atmosphere. People are genuine and eager to welcome newcomers. Children might be playing or running around after church, and people of all generations mingle. You might even receive an invitation to join a Sunday School class next week or a lunch invite from a few members who go out to eat. New Hope folks genuinely love guests.

In summary, when you visit New Hope Methodist Church, you should feel like you’ve **stepped into a community of friends**. The goal is for you to experience God’s love through the service and the people. If it’s your first time in a church (or first time in a long time), they’ll make every effort to help you feel comfortable. Come with an open heart, and feel free to ask questions or just observe. You’re welcome here\!

**Q: Do I have to be Methodist or a Christian to attend?**  
 **A:** Not at all\! New Hope welcomes *everyone*, regardless of background, faith, or denominational affiliation. You do not have to be a Methodist to attend services or events – in fact, many people who attend might come from various faith backgrounds (Catholic, Baptist, no church background at all, etc.). If you are not a Christian or are just exploring faith, you are especially welcome to come and learn at your own pace. The church community is respectful and happy to have you whether you’re firm in your beliefs or just curious. There’s no pressure to make any commitment; you won’t be put on the spot. If and when you have questions about Christianity or Methodism, the pastors would be glad to talk with you one-on-one. Essentially, **“come as you are and join us”** – New Hope is open to all who walk through its doors.
